The LA County Process

How a DCFS Case Unfolds at Edelman Court

Understanding each stage of the LA County dependency process puts you in a stronger position. Here is what to expect — and where our attorneys make the critical difference.

1

DCFS Referral & Investigation

A mandated reporter or concerned party contacts DCFS. A social worker is assigned to investigate. Do not give detailed statements without speaking to an attorney first — anything you say can be used in the petition.

2

Detention Hearing — Within 72 Hours

If your child was removed, you have a right to a hearing at Edelman Children's Court within 72 hours. A judge decides whether your child remains detained or comes home. The right attorney at this hearing can change everything.

3

Jurisdiction Hearing — 15 to 30 Days

The court reviews the DCFS petition and determines whether the allegations are true. Social worker reports, police reports, medical records, and witness testimony are all at issue. We challenge the petition aggressively.

4

Disposition Hearing

If jurisdiction is found, the court sets the case plan — services, visitation, and reunification timeline. We fight for terms that reflect your family's actual circumstances, not DCFS's default recommendations.

5

Review Hearings & Reunification

Every six months the court reviews your progress. Strong performance and strong advocacy at review hearings can dramatically accelerate reunification. We prepare you thoroughly for every appearance.
