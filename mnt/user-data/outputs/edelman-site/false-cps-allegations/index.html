How False Allegations Happen

A False Report to DCFS Can Destroy a Family — Even Without Evidence

In Los Angeles County, DCFS investigates every report it receives — including reports that are entirely fabricated, motivated by personal animus, or based on a genuine but completely mistaken interpretation of a child's circumstances. The sheer volume of referrals DCFS handles means that social workers often have limited time to investigate thoroughly before making consequential decisions.

False allegations are more common than many people realize. They arise in the context of bitter custody battles, contentious divorces, conflicts between neighbors or relatives, and mandated reporters — teachers, doctors, social workers — who report ambiguous situations out of an abundance of caution or genuine misinterpretation.

The critical fact: even a completely false DCFS referral can result in an investigation, a petition, a child removal, and a case at Edelman Children's Court. The allegations do not have to be true to set this process in motion. And once it is in motion, you need a skilled attorney who knows how to challenge it from the very beginning.

Common Sources of False Allegations
  • Custody disputes where one parent uses DCFS as a weapon
  • Divorce proceedings with contested child arrangements
  • Teachers or school staff misinterpreting normal childhood injuries
  • Medical providers over-reporting ambiguous findings
  • Neighbors or relatives acting on personal conflicts
  • Former partners motivated by retaliation
  • Cultural or religious practices misunderstood by mandated reporters
  • Mental health professionals over-reporting based on child disclosures
Our Defense Strategy

How We Challenge False DCFS Allegations

Exposing false allegations requires systematic investigation, evidence gathering, and aggressive legal advocacy. Here is our approach.

🔍

Investigate the Referral Source

We dig into who made the report, when they made it, and what their relationship is to your family. Reports made during custody proceedings, by estranged relatives, or by parties with personal motives are given particular scrutiny.

📂

Challenge the DCFS Investigation

We examine the DCFS investigation itself — who was interviewed, what questions were asked, whether proper procedures were followed, and whether the social worker's conclusions are supported by the actual evidence gathered.

🧾

Gather Independent Evidence

We collect independent evidence that contradicts DCFS's characterization — medical records, school records, photographs, text messages, witness statements, and expert opinions where appropriate.

👨‍⚖️

Cross-Examine DCFS Witnesses

At the jurisdiction hearing, we rigorously cross-examine DCFS social workers and other witnesses — exposing inconsistencies, procedural failures, and assumptions that undermine the credibility of the allegations.

📋

Challenge CACI Listing

If DCFS has substantiated allegations and listed you on the Child Abuse Central Index, we challenge that listing at a CACI grievance hearing — fighting to clear your record and protect your employment and future.

🛡️

Move for Dismissal

Where the evidence supports it, we move for full dismissal of the petition before or at the jurisdiction hearing. Our goal is not just a favorable case plan — it is complete vindication for families who have been wrongly targeted by DCFS.
