What Is the Detention Hearing

The 72-Hour Hearing That Determines Whether Your Child Comes Home Tonight

Under California law, when DCFS removes a child from a home, you have an absolute right to a detention hearing within 72 hours. This hearing takes place at Edmund D. Edelman Children's Court in Monterey Park, and it is the single most time-sensitive moment in the dependency process.

At the detention hearing, a judge — not a social worker, not a DCFS supervisor — reviews whether your child's continued removal is legally justified. DCFS presents its detention report. You have the right to respond, present evidence, and have an attorney argue on your behalf.

If the arguments are persuasive and the evidence supports it, the judge can order your child returned home immediately — while the broader case continues. This happens. It happens more often when parents have experienced, prepared counsel.

The DCFS detention report is often prepared quickly and may be incomplete, one-sided, or based on mischaracterized information. An attorney who reviews the report before the hearing, identifies its weaknesses, and presents counter-evidence can change the outcome in a way that no parent appearing alone can.

Time Is Critical

The detention hearing is often scheduled within 24–48 hours of removal. If you do not have an attorney by then, you will face the hearing alone — while DCFS is represented by the County Counsel.
