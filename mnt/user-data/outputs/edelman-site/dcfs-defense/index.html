Understanding DCFS

What DCFS Is, How It Works, and Why You Need a Defense Attorney

The Los Angeles County Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS) is the government agency responsible for investigating allegations of child abuse, neglect, and endangerment throughout Los Angeles County. It is, by far, the largest child welfare agency in the United States — processing hundreds of thousands of referrals every year.

When DCFS opens an investigation on your family, it assigns a social worker who will attempt to interview you, your child, other household members, and collateral contacts such as teachers or medical providers. The social worker has significant authority — including, in emergency situations, the ability to remove a child from the home without a court order.

DCFS is not a neutral party. Its social workers are trained investigators, and the agency is represented at Edelman Children's Court by the Los Angeles County Counsel's office — experienced attorneys who litigate these cases every day. You deserve the same level of preparation and experience on your side.

Know Your Rights

Your Rights During a DCFS Investigation

Many parents are unaware of their legal rights when DCFS investigates. Knowing these rights — and exercising them — can significantly affect the outcome of your case.

Right to Remain Silent

You are not required to answer detailed questions about your family or your child without legal representation. Be respectful — but politely decline substantive questions until you have spoken with an attorney.

Right to an Attorney

Parents facing juvenile dependency proceedings have the right to legal representation at all court hearings. If you cannot afford an attorney, the court may appoint one. However, private counsel with Edelman Court experience provides a critical advantage.

Right to a Detention Hearing

If DCFS removes your child, you are entitled to a detention hearing at Edelman Court within 72 hours. At that hearing, a judge — not a social worker — decides whether your child remains detained.

Right to Contest Allegations

The allegations in a DCFS petition are not proven facts. You have the right to contest them at the jurisdiction hearing, present your own evidence, and cross-examine DCFS witnesses.

Right to Notice

DCFS must provide you with notice of all hearings. You have the right to be present at every hearing that affects your family's case.

Right to Visitation

Even when a child is removed, parents generally have the right to visitation. We advocate for the maximum visitation time possible — and fight any DCFS attempts to restrict contact without justification.
